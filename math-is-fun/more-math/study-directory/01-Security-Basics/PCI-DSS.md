PCI DSS compliance is the process of following the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS). PCI DSS is a set of guidelines that protect cardholder data. 
Who needs to comply? 
Merchants that accept or process payment cards
Payment processors
Service providers that store, process, or transmit cardholder data
Banks that issue credit cards on behalf of major credit card companies
What does PCI DSS compliance involve? 
Securing networks with firewalls
Using strong passwords
Encrypting data
Protecting against malware
Maintaining secure systems and applications
Implementing access control measures
Regularly monitoring and testing networks
Maintaining an information security policy
Educating personnel on security policies
Why is PCI DSS compliance important? 
PCI DSS compliance helps ensure that credit card information remains secure
PCI DSS compliance fosters trust and security in payment transactions
Who develops PCI DSS? 
The PCI Security Standards Council (PCI SSC) is a global forum that develops and drives adoption of PCI DSS
